Lower Bounds of Hausdorff Density

It is known from an argument of Besicovitch that for a set $A \subset \mathbb{R}^{n}$ such that $H^{s}(A) < \infty$, $$ 2^{-s} \leq \Theta^{*s}(A,x) \leq 1$$ for almost every point in $A$. For example, see Mattila's Geometry of Sets and Measures in Euclidean Space, Chapter 6.
What we seek to show here is an example that the lower bound cannot be increased. To do so, we will construct a generalized Cantor set appropraitely as follows: Begin with $B(0,1) \subseteq \mathbb{R}^{n}$ and pick some $s>0$. We define some ratio $ \lambda \in (0,1)$ and some number $m_{\lambda}$ such that $m(\lambda^{k+1})^s = \lambda^{s}$. The ambient space $\R^{n}$ is chosen based on $\lambda$ to allow $m$ balls of radius $\lambda$ to exist in $B(0,1)$ (is this possible?).
We form levels $E_{k}$ as follows: $E_{1} = B(0,1)$, and $E_{k+1}$ is chosen by picking $m$ balls of radius $\lambda^{k+1}$ balls inside of each ball present in $E_{k}$. We shall pick these balls in the same equally distributed configuration in each ball of $E_{k}$. It is known (see Mattila's book again, Chapter 4) that such a construction allows a set $A_{\lambda} = \bigcap_{k=1}^{\infty} E_{k}$ such that $0< H^{s}(A_{\lambda}) < \infty$. By our choice, the set is also self-similar to its pieces. By appriapriate rescaling, assume $H^{s}(A_{\lambda}) = 1$.
Next, we compute a bound on the Hausdorff Density. For a point $x \in A_{\lambda}$, and a given $r > 0$ small, we may find $k$ such that $\lambda^{k+1} \leq r \leq \lambda^{k}$. Notice that $x$ is contained within a ball of radius $\lambda^{k}$ in level $E_{k}$, and in particular, $B(x,r)$ contains at-most $m$ balls of radius $\lambda^{k+1}$. Hence,
$\frac{H^{s}(A_{\lambda} \cap B(x,r))}{(2r)^{s}} \leq \frac{m (\lambda^{k+1})^{s} H^{s}(A)}{2^{s}r^{s}} \leq \left( \frac{\lambda^{k}}{r} \right)^{s} 2^{-s} \leq \lambda^{-s}2^{-s}$
As this holds everywhere in the set, we have $\Theta^{*s}(A_{\lambda},x) \leq \lambda^{-s}2^{-s}$. Since $\lambda$ was picked arbitrarily, we may see a variety of sets so that $\lambda \to 1$.
